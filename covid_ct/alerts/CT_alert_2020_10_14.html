EIDA/Covid in CT Alert (10/14/2020)

Based on data provided by CovidActNow (https://covidactnow.org/), the overall COVID risk of COVID-19 has increased from Medium to High. Connecticut is at risk of an outbreak.

CovidActNow tracks risk across 5 categories:
  • Daily new cases: this is the daily number of new cases per 100,00 individuals, averaged over the last week; a value > 1 indicates that the virus is currently not being contained.
  • Infection rate: this is the average number of people that an infected individual infects; a value < 1 indicates that the number of infections is decreasing.
  • Positive test rate: this is the percent of people tested who are positive for COVID-19; experts recommend that this value be < 3% to ensure that a sufficient amount of testing is occuring.
  • ICU headroom used: this is the percent of ICU beds in use; a value < 50% indicates that there is sufficient ICU capacity in the event of another wave of infections.
  • Tracers hired: this is the percent of contacts traced within 48 hours; a value over 90% is recommended for COVID to be contained, and indicates that enough tracers have been hired.

In the past day there has been an increase in risk for the following categories:

  • The risk level for Infection Rate has increased from Medium (1.05) to High (1.23)

The values for the other categories are as follows:

  • Daily New Cases: 9.0 (Medium)
  • Positive Test Rate: 1.3% (Low)
  • ICU Headroom: 11% (Low)
  • Contact Tracing: 53% (Medium)
